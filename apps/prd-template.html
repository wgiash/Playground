PRD Template

Aither · fill-in-the-blank

Index

Cover

Product name.

The opening frame — product title, PRD version, date, owner, and design lead.

Definition

What is this.

A paragraph stating what the product is, who it's for, and the one-line promise.

Problem

Why this exists.

Two to four bullets naming the specific pain or market gap this product addresses. Concrete, not generic.

Vision

Where we're going.

A paragraph on the three-to-five year vision — the aspirational end-state, not the v1 scope.

Ecosystem

Where this sits.

How this product relates to its sibling products, if part of a suite. A short paragraph and a simple diagram.

Phases

Now, next, later.

Three columns — v1 must-ship, v1.5 near-term, v2+ deferred. Three to five bullets each. Trade-offs made explicit.

Personas

Who we're designing for.

One to three personas for v1. For each: who they are, what they want, what they already buy, and the onboarding emphasis line.

Mental model

What this feels like.

The existing references the user already knows, composed into the product's mental model. "X meets Y." A short paragraph.

Vocabulary

The words we use.

The four to eight nouns this product introduces. One-line definition per noun. Used consistently throughout.

Principles

Rules we design against.

Five to eight non-negotiable design principles. Each: a short bold phrase plus one sentence. Every decision traces back to one.

Journey

The arc.

The user's journey from first contact to steady state. Key moments labeled — onboarding, first artifact, retention moment — and how surfaces unfold over time.

Flow

First visit.

The screens and decisions a brand-new user walks through on their first session, with drop-off risks called out.

Voice

How the product speaks.

Five to seven voice rules with two paired examples — one good, one anti-example. Gag words and accent words named.

Notifications

When we reach out.

Default pushes, opt-in pushes, the never-ship list, and quiet hours.

Surface

Beyond the app.

Any hardware, extension, integration, or out-of-app surface — states and affordances. Mark N/A if none.

Edges

Quiet failures.

Five to eight edge cases with their copy. Never shaming. Dignified on low-data weeks, pauses, deletions.

Settings

Trust, data, control.

Every setting the user can touch — data export, delete, notification prefs, account controls.

Scope

What we're not designing.

Explicitly deferred features with the version or reason each is deferred. Protects the ship date.

Questions

What to bring back.

Five to ten decisions for design to lead on. Each: one question plus what a "good answer" looks like.

Metrics

How we'll know it worked.

Five to seven measurable outcomes for v1, what a good number looks like, and what we won't optimize for.

Mood

Tone and references.

Products, films, writing, visual, audio. Anti-references — what this product is explicitly not.

Close

Next steps.

Owner, design lead, review cadence, and what happens after sign-off.
