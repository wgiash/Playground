Overview

Executive overview.

For anyone encountering a Sunspell product spec. Read this once, then read only the Summary at the top of each spec to stay synced.

The problem

A 3–4 person team shipping multiple MVPs (Sunpull, Sunrun, Sundial, Sunspoke — plus cult products) can't afford ambiguous docs. Handoffs, meetings, and re-litigation are all expensive. Every person needs to be able to go deep from the spec without blocking anyone else.

The system

Seven documents per product. One DRI each. Section-by-section signoff with auto-reset on edit. Every spec opens with a one-page Summary that everyone reads; the rest is build fuel.

How you use it

  1. Read all 6 Summary layers (≈6 min total) to stay synced across the team.
  2. Read your DRI spec in full to do your work.
  3. Drop a signed spec into Claude. It becomes prompt fuel — Wayeez builds UI from signed Design + Brand specs; Michael builds backend from signed Tech Spec.

What unlocks what

Master → Product → (Brand + Tech in parallel) → (Design + Website in parallel) → build → launch.

Rationale

Why this template exists.

The contract that lets Jasmine, Wayeez, and Michael each go deep in their own lane without blocking on each other. When a spec is signed, its DRI can disappear for a week and build.

It optimizes for three things, in order:

  1. No questions at build time. Spec is exhaustive where it matters.
  2. Deep-work feasibility. Each spec is self-sufficient once prerequisites are signed.
  3. Portfolio efficiency. Redundant content lives once (Master PRD), not N times.
Principles

Template design principles.

Seven rules that shaped how this template is structured. Every spec, every slide, obeys them.

  1. One DRI per doc. No co-review ambiguity. Reviewers comment; DRI decides.
  2. Three specs per product, not one PRD. Product Spec parents Design + Tech.
  3. Template standardizes the questions, not the answers. Every SUN is bespoke.
  4. Two gears: framing is short, spec is exhaustive. Don't mix registers.
  5. Section-by-section signoff, auto-reset on edit. Signoff can't silently stale.
  6. Deep work possible. Self-sufficient specs + explicit prerequisites.
  7. Traceability over trust. Every feature threads through Product → Design → Tech → Metrics.
Context

Portfolio structure.

How Sunspell's products relate. Every product uses the same 7-spec template. Cult products may mark SUN-specific slides (e.g., Cross-Product Interop) as N/A.

Architecture

Document architecture.

Seven specs per product, arranged by dependency. Signing a parent spec unlocks its children. Scope change mid-build → dependent specs pause → propagate up → re-branch.

MASTER SUNSPELL PRD      Jasmine · portfolio map       10 slides · 15 min
   ↓
PRODUCT SPEC             Jasmine · what & why          16 slides · 20 min
   ↓
   ├── BRAND SPEC        Wayeez · identity & voice     14 slides · 20 min
   │      ↓
   │      ├── DESIGN SPEC   Wayeez · flows & surfaces   15 slides · 30 min
   │      └── WEBSITE SPEC  Wayeez · marketing site     13 slides · 20 min
   ├── TECH SPEC         Michael · architecture         14 slides · 30 min
   └── ROADMAP SPEC      Jasmine · weekly tasks         8 slides · 10 min

Brand · 1 · Identity

Cover.

Name, version, DRI, link to Product Spec, prereq check.

Brand · 2 · Identity

Positioning line.

One sentence. The thing the product is. Passes the logo-removed test: if someone saw a touchpoint with no logo, would they still know it's this product?

Brand · 3 · Identity

Archetype.

Personality as an imagined quote. e.g., "The friend who always remembers what you liked." One sentence compresses personality better than a values list.

Brand · 4 · Identity

NOT list.

4–5 negative positioning statements. "Not a tech brand." "Not a luxury brand." Negative positioning is a first-class artifact — defines against, not just for.

Brand · 5 · Voice

Brand principles.

5 numbered. Format: noun-phrase + foil. e.g., 01 Human First (Real > polished); 02 Emotion Over Production (Imperfect is better). Principles are paired with the tension they resolve — never abstract virtues alone.

Brand · 6 · Voice

Tone.

4 adjectives, last one negative. e.g., "Warm, inclusive, slightly intimate, never try-hard." The negative forces the author to define against something.

Brand · 7 · Voice

House phrases.

3–5 reusable hero taglines. e.g., "This is from all of us." Provide the Claude prompt fuel for consistent copy generation.

Brand · 8 · Voice

Microcopy patterns.

Before/after table. Transactional copy → brand-voice copy. e.g., "Record response" → "Say something back". Teaches the translation rule, not just final copy.

Brand · 9 · System

Microcopy classes.

Canonical copy per state: empty · loading · error · success · permission-denied · rate-limited. Claude pulls from here for every flow.

Brand · 10 · System

Logo & spatial logic.

Usage rules. Forbidden treatments. Absolute don'ts ("Never X"), no "prefer" language.

Brand · 11 · System

Color.

Per color: name / role / one-sentence rationale. Rationale is the critical column — "Reads 'alive' without 'urgent'" — not just swatches.

Brand · 12 · System

Typography.

Font roles + absolute don'ts. e.g., "Never mix serif and sans at the same size."

Brand · 13 · System

Imagery & attitude.

Photography / illustration / motion stance. Brand attitude, not implementation detail.

Brand · 14 · Verification

Falsifiable tests.

3–5 tests that determine if the brand is working. e.g., "Logo-removed recognition." "Single-CTA rule holds." "Never paywall the message." Assertions that could be disproved — makes the Brand Spec usable in review, not aspirational.

Mechanics

Signoff ritual.

Weekly 30-min review. 1–2 slides per session. 3 slides/week/person is realistic. Async default: comments in doc; meeting only if unresolved. Any edit to a Signed slide flips status to In Review.

Mechanics

Decision log & open questions.

Open Questions: unresolved — blocks signoff. Decision Log: resolved — records decision + date + rationale + who decided. Prevents re-litigation. Format: YYYY-MM-DD · Decision · Rationale · Decided by. Changelog logs every edit to a Signed slide — audit trail for why signoff reset.

Mechanics

What this template does NOT cover.

Scope kept honest for a small team shipping MVPs. Re-evaluate each at P2+.

  • Responsive breakpoints — add at P1.5 when real product exists
  • Motion / transition spec — polish layer, post-launch
  • Disaster recovery — MVP-too-early
  • CI/CD + build/deploy detail — lives in infra, not spec
  • Financing / runway — not a product doc's job
  • Detailed copy library — per-product, lives in content tool, linked from Voice slide
  • API reference docs — generated from code, not hand-specified
